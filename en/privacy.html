Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026 · This is a translation of the Dutch privacy policy, which is the authoritative version.

RightSetFret is offered by PLAN B EBVBA (Boeyenstraat 10, 3600 Genk, Belgium). We value your privacy and collect as little data as possible. This policy explains which data is processed and why.

Your account

You sign in with Sign in with Apple. We store a stable account identifier provided by Apple and — only if you choose to share it — your name. This data is used solely to recognise your account. Your email address is managed by Apple; you can choose to hide it.

Your recipes and cookbooks

Your recipes, cookbooks and meal plans are stored in your own iCloud (Apple CloudKit, private database) under your Apple ID, and sync across your devices. This data lives in your iCloud; we have no access to it.

Shared shopping list

If you choose to share a shopping list, that list is stored in a shared iCloud space (CloudKit public database), linked to a family code you choose. Anyone who knows the same code can view and edit that list. Only share your code with people you trust.

Sharing recipes with your family

You can choose to share your saved recipes with your family. This is off by default and you turn it on yourself with a single switch in Settings. When on, your recipes (text and a downscaled photo) are stored in the same family-code-protected shared space as the shopping list, so family members with the same code can view them and, if they wish, copy them into their own collection. You choose your own family display name. The full-resolution photo stays in your own iCloud; only a downscaled version is shared. If you turn sharing off, or sign out or delete your account, your shared recipes are removed from that space. As with the shopping list: only share your family code with people you trust.

Importing recipes

When you import a recipe (a link, pasted text, or an image), that content is sent to our server service to turn it into a recipe. An external AI service (Anthropic Claude) is used to structure the text. For social media links, the server fetches the publicly available content of the post (caption, cover image, audio) to read the recipe. This content is processed only to fulfil your request and is not kept longer than necessary.

Usage statistics

To improve RightSetFret we collect anonymous usage statistics using PostHog. This data is processed on servers in the European Union and is never used to identify you.

We measure which features are used — for example how often a recipe is imported or a meal plan is created — along with a few general characteristics of the installation: your device’s language and region setting, the app version, the chosen theme, whether you have a subscription, and roughly how many recipes your collection holds (in broad brackets, such as “25–49”). Your installation is given a random number so that individual events can be grouped together. That number is not linked to your name, email address or Apple account.

What we do not collect: your recipes, photos, notes and shopping lists. Those stay on your device and in your own iCloud. We also do not track you across other apps or websites, and we do not share this data with advertisers.

Subscriptions and payments

Subscriptions and purchases are handled entirely by Apple (App Store). We do not receive or store any payment details. Managing and cancelling your subscription happens via your Apple ID settings.

What we don't do

We do not sell your personal data and do not share it with advertisers.

External services

  • Apple — Sign in with Apple, iCloud storage and App Store payments
  • Anthropic — AI processing for recognising recipes
  • PostHog — anonymous usage statistics (processed in the EU)
  • Fly.io — hosting of our server service

Your rights

You can access, correct or delete your data. You can delete your recipes in iCloud yourself in the app. If you want your account and associated data erased, please contact us. Under the GDPR you have the right to access, rectification, erasure and objection.

Children

RightSetFret is not aimed at children and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

We may update this policy. We will announce significant changes in the app or on this page.
